A boids experiment

Boids ("bird-oids") is a simulation of flocking introduced in a paper by Craig Reynolds in 1987. It's an agent-based model where the computer simulates each individual bird using some simple rules, producing a complex flocking behaviour.

The three rules are:

  • Birds will try to face in the same direction as birds near them, so they can move as a flock
  • They'll try to keep a separation distance from each other, so they don't fly into each other
  • They'll try to keep as close as they can to the middle of the flock, so they don't fly off alone

In this experiment, you're going to play with how strong these different rules are to see what happens to the flock.
